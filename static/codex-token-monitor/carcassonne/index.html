How to Play

Objective

Build the land tile by tile. Score points by completing roads, cities, and monasteries. Place your followers (meeples) strategically to claim features.

Game Flow

  1. Draw a tile - A random tile is drawn from the deck.
  2. Rotate & place - Green-highlighted cells show valid placements. Click to place. Press R to rotate.
  3. Place a meeple (optional) - Click a feature (road, city, monastery, or field) on the just-placed tile. Cannot claim a feature already claimed by anyone.
  4. End turn - Completed features are scored automatically. Meeples return to your supply.

Controls

ClickPlace tile on highlighted cell
RRotate tile 90 degrees

DragPan the board
ScrollZoom in/out

Scoring

  • Road (completed): 1 point per tile
  • City (completed): 2 points per tile + 2 per shield
  • Monastery (completed): 9 points when surrounded by 8 tiles
  • End-game incomplete roads: 1 point per tile
  • End-game incomplete cities: 1 point per tile + 1 per shield
  • End-game monasteries: 1 point per surrounding tile + 1 for the monastery itself
  • End-game fields: 3 points per completed city bordering the field

About this Edition

This is a hot-seat prototype for 2-5 players. The tile distribution is a representative deck (approximately 40 tiles) rather than the official 72-tile set. Core object rules and scoring mechanics are preserved. No AI or network play. All rendering is procedural (no original artwork used).
